How you can turn your customers into fans...

Let me lead your team to excellence as a Product Owner or Digital Product Manager.

1. Ideation Process

"Start with 'WHY?'. Fall in love with the problem, not with the solution."(1) Gather evdidence on real pains and gains of your customers and get an overview of the market you are in. Based on methods like Design Thinking we discover the problem space of your (potential) customers and collect first ideas how to serve them best.

2. Design (Sprint)

As part of the Design Sprint we dive deep into the problems spaces of your core customers.(2) Using methods like Customer Journey Mapping we identify the most promising solutions at every touchpoint of their user experience. As a result ideas are clear enough to start prototyping for your core costomers.

3. Prototyping

Why spend money for development, before you know it provides value? Increase velocity and value and reduce risc of failour at same with prototyping.(3) Get priceless feedback to a prototype BEFORE development. Be SURE in a fraction of time your core customers LOVE your product and are willing to pay for it.

4. Empower Teams

Outcome over output. Whithin a two week period I can lead your team deliver small solutions striving to answer the same four questions repeatedly: Will your customer buy this? Does he know how to use this intuitivly? How do we build this reliable & flexible? Is this viable for you business? (4)

5. Scope. Time. Quality. Budget.

Following phases 1. to 4. the scope of the product is in perfect alignment with your core customers needs. Time: Every two weeks you can look forward to a new peace of pure value. Quality is NOT negotiable but time- and cost-saving. (5) Resultingly your budget is in safe hands at full transparency of cost on a 2-week basis.

6. Monitoring. Feedback. Progress.

Take the chance in the two-weekly SCRUM Review Meeting to 'inspect & adapt' the results your SCRUM Team achieved. Take steps in the right direction and give your SCRUM Team feedback on a regular basis.(6) There is no better way to foster accelerated progress and deliver time to market.
